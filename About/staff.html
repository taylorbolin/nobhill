Team

Aaron Gordon

Owner

After his first construction project, a tree house when he was a kid, Aaron knew he was onto something. In the time between that tree house and founding AGC Aaron worked for his father manufacturing speakers in the car audio industry. There he developed his instincts for business, and went on to earn a business degree from Sonoma State University to greaten his knowledge of business. Following graduation he worked as a stage hand and set builder. During this time he started doing small construction projects in his spare time. These became larger in scope and more frequent until Aaron eventually decided to get his general contractor’s license and combine his passion for building with his passion for running a business. He has never looked back. When he’s not working Aaron spends time with his wife and son, goes camping, plays tennis, and thinks business.


Josh Grout

Project Development

Josh is the primary contact for AGC clients. He is their guide, resource, and advocate throughout all project phases, from design to completion. Having grown up in construction, Josh’s father being a contractor and the family home perennially under construction, he has been managing projects and expectations since he was 20 years old. His network includes some of the best architects, designers, and engineers throughout the Bay Area and he has a knack for assembling effective teams to carry out what is best suited for AGC’s clients. In his free time, he can be found playing with his children and searching for a great trail to run.


Brian Dutro

Operations Manager

Brian leads the team that creates project budgets and schedules. Before construction he works with clients, architects, designers, and the AGC team to align client expectations, architect vision, and construction logistics to prepare for successful projects. During construction he works with the AGC production department to see through those logistics. Outside of AGC, Brian enjoys getting outside, reading, writing, and raiding monasteries on the North Sea Coast.


Eric Aurandt

Production Manager

Eric got his start as an apprentice carpenter while attending graduate art school in New York, beginning a career that rolled together the physicality of his days of university rugby with the sensibilities he refined earning an MFA in sculpture. Now he leverages his years of experience and deep knowledge of building to oversee production at AGC. He coaches AGC foremen and crew, ensures construction quality, manages trade contractors, and makes sure challenges are anticipated and provisioned for before they can become problems. Outside of work Eric enjoys spending time with his wife and two sons, playing music, and making sculpture.


Brian Burns

Office Manager

As Chief Financial Officer, Brian oversees all financial obligations such as contracts, subcontracts, payroll, systems and software for all projects, he also regulates contracts between 415 Remodeling, subcontractors, vendors, and clients before a job begins. To keep 415 Remodeling running smoothly he tops off his responsibilities by managing all employee benefits from health insurance to 401K plans. When he isn’t balancing a budget Brian, California born and bred, can be found rooting for the Giants, 49ers, or Warriors teams.


Amber Stitt

Estimator

Amber received her undergraduate degree in International Studies at Cal State, East Bay. After college, she began working as a painter for a general contractor. She moved on to carpentry, after noticing that carpenters seemed most involved in the remodel process, experiencing a large variety of work. After working at AGC as a carpenter, it quickly became clear that her expertise and diligence would be suited for an estimator. As is the case with the best estimators, her hands-on carpentry experience informs her ability to estimate costs. In her free time, she follows and plays sports and performs with her band.
